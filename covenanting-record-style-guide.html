Section 01

Front Matter

Every file begins with a front matter block between --- markers. This tells Jekyll the title, date, layout, and other metadata.

Journal Post posts only 
---
layout: post
title: "Your Post Title Here"
date: 2026-04-23 10:00:00 +0000
excerpt: "One or two sentences summarising the post."
tags: [covenant, reformation, scotland]
comments: true
---
Historical Document _documents only 
---
layout: document
title: "The Solemn League and Covenant"
subtitle: "A Brief Introduction and Text"
description: "Short description for SEO."
order: 1
section: "Historical Documents"
comments: false
---
KeyRequired?Example valueNotes
layoutrequiredpost or documentUse post for journal entries; document for the sidebar layout
titlerequired"On the Remnant"Always wrap in double quotes. Do not repeat this as a heading in the body — the layout renders it automatically.
daterequired (posts)2026-04-23 10:00:00 +0000Must also appear in the filename: 2026-04-23-slug.md
excerptoptional"A brief summary…"Shown in the post list on the home page. Keep to 1–2 sentences.
tagsoptional[covenant, scotland]Lowercase, hyphenated. Square bracket list, no quotes needed.
commentsoptionaltrue or falseOmitting this defaults to showing comments on posts, hiding on documents.
subtitledocuments only"With Introduction"Renders in italic below the title.
orderdocuments only1Controls the sort order in the sidebar navigation. Lower numbers appear first.
sectiondocuments only"Historical Documents"Groups documents under a heading in the sidebar. Documents with the same section string are grouped together.
Section 02

Headings

Three heading levels are available. Note that H1 is reserved — the layout renders the front matter title as H1 automatically. Never add a # heading in the body that duplicates the title.

H2 — Section heading Large italic serif. Use for major divisions within a post.
Of the National Covenant
## Of the National Covenant
H3 — Sub-section heading Smaller italic serif. Use for subsections or article numbers.
Article the First
### Article the First
Remember: Never begin a post body with a ## Title that copies the front matter title — it will appear twice on the page.

Plain paragraph The default. Just write. Blank line = new paragraph.

Section 04

Scripture Blocks

Use the .scripture class for extended Scripture quotations. KJV italic words (translator's insertions) are marked with <em> tags. Include a <cite> for the reference.

.scripture Italic text with gold left border. The canonical way to quote Scripture.
And I will bring the third part through the fire, and will refine them as silver is refined, and will try them as gold is tried: they shall call on my name, and I will hear them: I will say, It is my people: and they shall say, The Lord is my God. Zechariah 13:9 
<div class="scripture">
And I will bring the third part through the fire, and will
refine them as silver is refined, and will try them as gold
is tried: they shall call on my name, and <em>I</em> will hear
them: <em>I</em> will say, It <em>is</em> my people: and they
shall say, The LORD <em>is</em> my God.
<cite>Zechariah 13:9</cite>
</div>
KJV italic words: In the Authorised Version, words printed in italics were added by the translators for clarity. Mark these with <em>word</em> to preserve this distinction on the site.

Section 06

Sidenotes

Sidenotes appear in the right margin on desktop, and expand on tap on mobile. Each sidenote needs a unique ID number. Critical rule: the three tags must be placed inside the paragraph, directly after the word being annotated, with no blank line before them and all on a single unbroken line.

Sidenote (numbered) Superscript number in text; note floats to right margin.
The Westminster Confession of Faith1 was adopted by the General Assembly of the Church of Scotland in 1647, and remains the subordinate standard of that church to this day.
The Confession was approved by the General Assembly on 27th August 1647.
The three tags must sit on ONE unbroken line, no blank line before them:

The Westminster Confession of Faith<label for="sn-1" class="margin-toggle sidenote-number"></label><input type="checkbox" id="sn-1" class="margin-toggle"/><span class="sidenote">Your note text here.</span>
was adopted by the General Assembly...

For the second sidenote on the page, change sn-1 to sn-2, and so on.
Common mistake: Leaving a blank line between the paragraph text and the <label> tag. This pushes the number onto a new line. The fix_sidenotes.py script corrects this automatically if needed.
Section 07

Margin Notes

Like sidenotes, but without a number. Use for supplementary asides that don't need to interrupt the reading flow — cross-references, dates, brief annotations.

Margin note (unnumbered) No superscript number. Right margin only. Uses a ⊕ toggle symbol on mobile.
The Covenanters suffered greatly under the Restoration settlement of 1660 and the years of the Killing Time that followed.
1660–1688. The period of Episcopalian persecution following the death of Cromwell.
Note: use mn-1, mn-2 etc. for margin note IDs (distinct from sn-1, sn-2)

Section 10

Dividers

A horizontal rule creates a short centred line (55% width). Useful between sections of a longer post.

Horizontal ruleShort centred decorative divider
---
(Three hyphens on their own line, with blank lines above and below)
Note: Three hyphens (---) also marks the end of the front matter block. Make sure there is a blank line above a divider, so Jekyll does not confuse it with front matter.

VerseClick automatically detects Scripture references in plain text and converts them to pop-up links. No special markup is needed. Simply write the reference naturally.

Automatic detection — formats that work

Only full Book-Chapter:Verse citations are linked.

References inside H1–H4 headings are intentionally not linked.
Section 13

Practical Tips

Tip 1 — Never repeat the title. The layout renders the front matter title as a large H1 automatically. Starting the body with ## Same Title will show the heading twice.
Tip 2 — Sidenotes must be inline. The sidenote <label><input><span> block must sit inside the paragraph, directly after the annotated word, on one unbroken line, with no blank line separating it from the preceding text. Run python fix_sidenotes.py if any look wrong.
Tip 3 — Asterisks in historical texts. If you paste text containing asterisks (e.g. *Anno Domini*), Markdown will render them as italic/bold. Either remove them or escape them with a backslash: \*Anno Domini\*.
Tip 4 — Post filename must match the date. The file must be named YYYY-MM-DD-slug-here.md and the date: field in the front matter must begin with the same YYYY-MM-DD.
Tip 5 — HTML within Markdown is fine. Jekyll processes Markdown files but passes raw HTML through unchanged. So you can mix plain Markdown paragraphs with <div class="scripture"> blocks freely — no special setting is needed.
Tip 6 — Closing Soli Deo Gloria. Posts conventionally close with an italic Soli Deo Gloria. on its own line, followed by the footer disclaimer if needed. Write it as:

*Soli Deo Gloria.*
