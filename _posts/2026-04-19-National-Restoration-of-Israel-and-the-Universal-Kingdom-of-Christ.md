**I.** As the Lord Jesus, in the withering of the fig tree, did judicially
terminate the carnal and typical administration of the Jewish theocracy for
its fruitlessness; so hath He nevertheless reserved a sovereign and covenantal
purpose for that nation in the ages to come. The present rejection of the
natural branches, though just and according to the decree of reprobation upon
that generation, is neither total nor final; for the Covenant made with the
fathers remaineth sure in the hand of the Mediator.

**II.** The Lord shall, in the season of His appointed Millennial glory, pour
out a Spirit of grace and national repentance upon the seed of Jacob. In this
work, the infinite merit of Christ's Person &mdash; He having once died as
the Corn of Wheat to purchase not only individuals but nations &mdash; shall
be most illustriously displayed. Israel, being sovereignly reclaimed from their
long dispersion and judicial hardness, shall look upon Him whom they have
pierced, and by a formal act of National Covenanting, shall swear allegiance
to the Crown Rights of King Jesus.

**III.** This receiving of Israel into the New Covenant fold shall be the
principal instrument and catalyst for the universal enlargement of the
Redeemer's Kingdom. For if the casting away of them was the occasion of the
reconciling of the world, much more shall their national restoration be as
&#8220;life from the dead&#8221; to the Gentile nations. It shall usher in a
state of the Church characterised by such purity, peace, and fruitfulness as
hath not yet been seen in the earth; wherein the knowledge of the Lord shall
cover the sea, and the nations of the world shall walk in the light of the
New Jerusalem.

**IV.** In that day, the barrenness of the old fig tree shall be forgotten in
the flourishing of the Olive; and the various kingdoms of the world, provoked
to holy emulation by the restoration of the Jews, shall themselves become the
kingdoms of our Lord and of His Christ. Thus shall the whole earth be brought
into a sworn, covenantal subjection to the Messiah, to the praise of His
glorious grace and the manifestation of His absolute sovereignty over the
nations of men.

## A Theological Note on the Covenanter View

In this framework, the &#8220;life from the dead&#8221; in Romans 11:15 is
viewed as a global spiritual resurrection. The Covenanters, such as Samuel
Rutherford and George Gillespie, often argued that just as Israel's fall led
to the Gospel being sent to the Gentiles, their return would act as a second
&#8220;Pentecost&#8221; for the whole world. The focus is not merely on the
salvation of individual Jews, but on the national and corporate body being
brought into a Covenant with God, which then serves as the blueprint for all
other nations to follow in National Covenanting.
